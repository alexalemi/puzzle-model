Overview

How to read these predictions: We simulate the entire tournament 20,000 times, drawing puzzle difficulties, competitor performances, and noise from the fitted model. Probabilities reflect how often each outcome occurs across simulations. Competitors not in our model database are labeled cold-start and assigned the population prior (average ability, high uncertainty). They collectively account for a large share of win probability because any of them could be an elite puzzler we haven't observed before.

Methodology

Predictions are generated by a Bayesian latent factor model fitted to ~245,000 historical speed-puzzling observations from speedpuzzling.com, myspeedpuzzling.com, and USA Jigsaw Association results. The model estimates ability parameters for ~11,000 puzzlers.

Model

Each solve time is modeled in milliBels (mB = 1000 × log10(seconds)) as:

log_time = μ + αi + βj + f(N) + ε

where αi is puzzler ability, βj is puzzle difficulty, f(N) corrects for piece count, and ε ~ w·Normal(0, σ) + (1−w)·StudentT(ν, 0, k·σ) captures noise (NST mixture).

For pairs/teams, individual alphas combine via logsumexp (rates add in parallel) with an Amdahl's law correction for serial work fraction and per-team-size residual terms.

Simulation

Each Monte Carlo trial draws: a shared puzzle β for the heat, individual α values from each competitor's posterior (or population prior for cold-start), and NST mixture noise (Normal core + Student-t tails). Times exceeding the limit are DNF. Top finishers advance; finals use fresh draws.

Cold-Start Competitors

About 25% of registered competitors have no historical data in our model. They receive the population prior: α ~ Normal(0, σα) with σα ≈ 219 mB. This means each unknown competitor could range from elite to novice. With ~200 unknowns, the extreme draws collectively dominate tail probabilities (wins, podiums). This reflects genuine uncertainty — not a flaw. The "Known Only" tab filters to competitors with model estimates.
