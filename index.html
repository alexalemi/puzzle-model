Speed Puzzling Model Explorer

A Bayesian latent factor model for predicting jigsaw puzzle competition times

Alexander A. Alemi Solo Explorer GitHub
About this page

This explorer presents a joint solo+team Bayesian model for jigsaw puzzle solving times. It extends the solo-only Model 3het with team mechanics: when multiple puzzlers work together, their individual rates combine via logsumexp (parallel rate addition), subject to Amdahl's law (serial fraction) and per-bucket residual corrections.

For solo solves (K=1), the team model reduces exactly to Model 3het — logsumexp of a single alpha is just that alpha, and all team corrections vanish. This means solo predictions are unchanged while team predictions benefit from the additional structure.

The model is fitted jointly on solo, duo, and group data from speedpuzzling.com, myspeedpuzzling.com, USA Jigsaw, and a personal dataset. Team members who also have solo data provide the key link — their individual ability estimates are informed by both solo and team observations.

Ratings are in milliBels (mB): 1000 mB = factor of 10 in time, 100 mB ≈ 26% change. Higher puzzler ratings mean faster solvers; higher puzzle ratings mean harder puzzles.

Predicting a solve time: Look up the puzzle's Difficulty (mB) and subtract your own puzzler mB rating. If the result is negative, expect to finish in under an hour; if positive, over an hour. The magnitude tells you how far: ±100 mB ≈ ±26% from one hour. If you've solved the puzzle before, subtract about 3 mB for each doubling of your solve count (the practice effect: γ·ln(solve #), where γ ≈ −4 mB). To convert to seconds, add μ and raise: 10(result + μ)/1000. Each value has an uncertainty σ — you can add or subtract it to get optimistic and pessimistic estimates. The Prediction Calculator supports team predictions — add multiple team members and see the combined prediction with Amdahl and eta corrections applied.

How to use this page: Prediction Calculator supports both solo and team predictions. Rankings shows puzzler and puzzle tables from the joint model. Data Overview summarizes the combined dataset. Model Specification describes the team formula. For technical details and the autoresearch story, see Autoresearch Explorer.
